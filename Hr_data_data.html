HR Analytics Project â€“ Synthetic HR Datasets

This project creates synthetic HR-related datasets to simulate common business questions in people analytics, such as pay equity, employee engagement, turnover prediction, recruiting source effectiveness, performance evaluation, and workplace safety.

The generated CSV files can be used for: - Exploratory HR data analysis
- Predictive modeling (e.g., churn/attrition prediction)
- Dashboard creation in R Shiny, Tableau, or Power BI
- Portfolio projects to showcase HR analytics capabilities

Recruitment data

(Hiring & Sales Quota)

  • Variables:

    • attrition (Yes/No)

    • performance_rating (1-5)

    • sales_quota_pct (0.3 = 30% to 2.5 = 250% of target)

    • recruiting_source

set.seed(105)
n <- 500
attrition <- sample(c("Yes","No"), n, TRUE)
performance_rating <- sample(1:5, n, TRUE)
sales_quota_pct <- round(runif(n, min = 0.3, max = 2.5), 3)
recruiting_source <- sample(c("Applied Online","Campus","Referral ","Search Firm","N/A"), n, TRUE)

recruitment_data <- data.frame(attrition, performance_rating, sales_quota_pct, recruiting_source)
head(recruitment_data)
  attrition performance_rating sales_quota_pct recruiting_source
1       Yes                  1           0.494         Referral 
2        No                  5           0.883    Applied Online
3        No                  3           1.302    Applied Online
4        No                  3           2.140         Referral 
5       Yes                  4           1.560         Referral 
6       Yes                  3           2.074    Applied Online
tail(recruitment_data)
    attrition performance_rating sales_quota_pct recruiting_source
495        No                  4           1.594       Search Firm
496       Yes                  3           0.707               N/A
497        No                  1           0.398    Applied Online
498        No                  1           1.587       Search Firm
499        No                  3           1.421            Campus
500       Yes                  2           0.484       Search Firm
write.csv(recruitment_data, "recruitment_data.csv", row.names = FALSE)

Fair pay data

(Compensation & Job Level)

  • Variables:

    • employee_id

    • department

    • salary

    • new_hire (Yes/No)

    • job_level (Hourly, Salaried, Manager)

# ---- Fair Pay Data ----
set.seed(104)

fair_pay <- data.frame(employee_id, department, salary, new_hire, job_level)
